Visa requirements for Maltese citizens

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
Jump to: navigation, search

Visa requirements for Maltese citizens are administrative entry restrictions by the authorities of other states placed on citizens of Malta. In 2014, Maltese citizens had visa-free or visa on arrival access to 166 countries and territories, ranking the Maltese passport 8th in the world (the highest ranking of all the recent new member states of the EU).[1]

Territories[edit]

Visa requirements for Maltese citizens for visits to various territories, disputed areas, partially recognized countries and restricted zones:

Europe
  •  Abkhazia — Visa required.[203]
  • Mount Athos — Special permit required (4 days: 25 euro for Orthodox visitors, 35 euro for non-Orthodox visitors, 18 euro for students). There is a visitors' quota: maximum 100 Orthodox and 10 non-Orthodox per day and women are not allowed.[204][205]
  • Crimea — Visa issued by Russia is required.[206]
  •  Turkish Republic of Northern Cyprus — Visa free access for 3 months. Passport required.[207]
  • UN Buffer Zone in Cyprus — Access Permit is required for travelling inside the zone, except Civil Use Areas.[208]
  •  Faroe Islands — Visa not required.[209]
  •  Gibraltar — Visa not required.[210]
  •  Guernsey – Visa not required.[211]
  •  Alderney – Visa not required.[212]
  •  Sark – Visa not required.[213]
  •  Isle of Man — Visa not required.[214][215]
  • Jan Mayen — permit issued by the local police required for staying for less than 24 hours[216] and permit issued by the Norwegian police for staying for more than 24 hours.[217]
  •  Jersey – Visa not required.[218]
  •  Kosovo — visa free for 90 days.[219]
  •  Nagorno-Karabakh Republic — Visa required (issued for single entry for 21 days/1/2/3 months or multiple entry visa for 1/2/3 months).[220][221] Travellers with Nagorno-Karabakh visa (expired or valid) or evidence of travel to Nagorno-Karabakh (stamps) will be permanently denied entry to Azerbaijan.[222]
  • Closed cities and regions in Russia — special authorization required.[223]
  •  South Ossetia — Visa free. Multiple entry visa to Russia and three day prior notification are required to enter South Ossetia.[224][225]
  •  Transnistria — Visa free. Registration required after 24h.[226][227]
Africa
  •  Eritrea (outside Asmara) — visa covers Asmara only; to travel in the rest of the country, a Travel Permit for Foreigners is required (20 Eritrean nakfa).[228]
  •  Sahrawi Arab Democratic Republic (Western Sahara controlled territory) — Visa not required up to 3 months.[229]
  •  Somaliland — Visa issued on arrival (30 days for 30 US dollars, payable on arrival).[230][231]
  •  Sudan — All foreigners traveling more than 25 kilometers outside of Khartoum must obtain a travel permit.
  • Darfur — Separate travel permit is required.[232]
Asia
  •  Hong Kong — Visa not required for 90 days.[233]
  •  India — Protected Area Permit (PAP) required for whole states of Nagaland and Sikkim and parts of states Manipur, Arunachal Pradesh, Uttaranchal, Jammu and Kashmir, Rajasthan, Himachal Pradesh. Restricted Area Permit (RAP) required for all of Andaman and Nicobar Islands and parts of Sikkim. Some of these requirements are occasionally lifted for a year.[234][235][236][237][238][239]
  •  Iraqi Kurdistan — Visa on arrival for 15 days is available at Erbil and Sulaymaniyah airports.[240]
  •  Kazakhstan — Special permission required for the town of Baikonur and surrounding areas in Kyzylorda Oblast, and the town of Gvardeyskiy near Almaty.[241]
  • Kish Island — Visitors to Kish Island do not require a visa.[242]
  •  Macao — Visa not required for 90 days.[243]
  • Sabah and Sarawak — These states have their own immigration authorities and passport is required to travel to them, however the same visa applies.[244]
  • Maldives — With the exception of the capital Malé, tourists are generally prohibited from visiting non-resort islands without the express permission of the Government of Maldives.[245]
  •  Palestine — Visa not required. Arrival by sea to Gaza Strip not allowed.[246][247]
  •  Taiwan — Visa not required for 90 days.[248]
  • Gorno-Badakhshan Autonomous Province — OIVR permit required (15+5 Tajikistani Somoni) and another special permit (free of charge) is required for Lake Sarez.[249][250][251]
  •  Turkmenistan — A special permit, issued prior to arrival by Ministry of Foreign Affairs, is required if visiting the following places: Atamurat, Cheleken, Dashoguz, Serakhs and Serhetabat.[252]
  • Tibet Autonomous Region — Tibet Travel Permit required (10 US Dollars).[253][254][255]
  • Korean Demilitarized Zone — restricted zone.
  • UNDOF Zone and Ghajar — restricted zones.
  • Phú Quốc — can visit without a visa for up to 30 days.[256]
  •  Yemen — Special permission needed for travel outside Sana’a or Aden.[257]
Caribbean and North Atlantic
  •  Anguilla — Visa not required for 3 months.[258][259]
  •  Aruba — Visa not required for 30 days.[260]
  • Bonaire, St. Eustatius and Saba — Visa not required for 3 months.[261]
  •  Bermuda — Visa not required for 21 days (extendable).[262][263]
  •  British Virgin Islands — Visa not required for 1 month (extendable).[264][265]
  •  Cayman Islands — Visa not required for 6 months.[266][267]
  •  Colombia — Visitors arriving at San Andrés and Leticia must buy tourist cards on arrival.[268]
  •  Curacao — Visa not required for 3 months.[269]
  •  Montserrat — Visa not required for 6 months.[270]
  •  Greenland — Visa not required.[271]
  • Margarita Island — All visitors are fingerprinted.[272]
  •  Puerto Rico — Visa not required under the Visa Waiver Program, for 90 days on arrival from overseas for 2 years. ESTA required.[273]
  •  Saint Pierre and Miquelon — Visa not required.[274]
  •  Sint Maarten — Visa not required for 3 months.[275]
  •  Turks and Caicos Islands — Visa not required for 90 days.[276][277]
  •  U.S. Virgin Islands — Visa not required under the Visa Waiver Program, for 90 days on arrival from overseas for 2 years. ESTA required.[273]
Oceania
  •  American Samoa — Visa required (entry permit).[278]
  • Ashmore and Cartier Islands — special authorisation required.[279]
  • Clipperton Island — special permit required.[280]
  •  Cook Islands — Visa free access for 31 days.[281]
  • Lau Province — Special permission required.[282]
  •  French Polynesia — Visa not required.[283]
  •  Guam — Visa not required under the Visa Waiver Program, for 90 days on arrival from overseas for 2 years. ESTA required.[273]
  •  New Caledonia — Visa not required for 3 months.[284]
  •  Niue — Visa on arrival valid for 30 days is issued free of charge.[285]
  •  Northern Mariana Islands — Visa not required.[286]
  •  Pitcairn Islands — 14 days visa free and landing fee 35 USD or tax of 5 USD if not going ashore.[287][288][289][290]
  • United States Minor Outlying Islands — special permits required for Baker Island, Howland Island, Jarvis Island, Johnston Atoll, Kingman Reef, Midway Atoll,Palmyra Atoll and Wake Island.[291][292][293][294][295][296][297]
  •  Wallis and Futuna — Visa not required.[298]
South Atlantic and Antarctica
  •  Falkland Islands — Visitor Permit valid for 4 weeks is issued on arriva.[299][300]
  •  Saint Helena, Ascension and Tristan da Cunha
    •  Ascension Island — Entry Permit must be obtained minimum 28 days in advance (3 months for 20/30 pounds sterling, single/double entry).[301]
    •  Saint Helena — Entry Permit (£25) for 183 days is issued on arrival.[302]
    •  Tristan da Cunha — Permission to land required for 15/30 pounds sterling (yacht/ship passenger) for Tristan da Cunha Island or 20 pounds sterling for Gough Island, Inaccessible Island or Nightingale Islands.[303]
  •  South Georgia and the South Sandwich Islands — Pre-arrival permit from the Commissioner required (72 hours/1 month for 110/160 pounds sterling).[304][305][306]
  • Antarctica and adjacent islands — special permits required for  French Southern and Antarctic Lands,  Argentine Antarctica, Australian Antarctic Territory, Chilean Antarctic Territory, Heard Island and McDonald Islands, Peter I Island, Queen Maud Land, Ross Dependency.[307][308][309][310][311][312][313][314][315][316][317]
  •  Arab League — Certain countries will deny access to holders of Israeli visas or passport stamps of Israel because of the Arab League boycott of Israel.

Vaccination[edit]

Many African countries, including Angola, Benin, Burkina Faso, Cameroon, Central African Republic, Chad, Democratic Republic of the Congo, Republic of the Congo, Côte d'Ivoire, Equatorial Guinea, Gabon, Ghana, Guinea, Liberia, Mali, Mauritania, Niger, Rwanda, São Tomé and Príncipe, Senegal, Sierra Leone, Uganda, Zambia require all incoming passengers to have a current International Certificate of Vaccination. Some other countries require vaccination only if the passenger is coming from an infected area.[318]

Passport validity[edit]

Many countries require passport validity of no less than 6 months and one or two blank pages.[319]

Countries requiring passport validity of at least 6 months on arrival include Afghanistan, Algeria, Bhutan, Botswana, Brunei, Cambodia, Comoros, Côte d'Ivoire, Ecuador, Egypt, El Salvador, Fiji, Guyana, Indonesia, Iran, Iraq (except when arriving at Basra - 3 months and Erbil or Sulaimaniyah - on arrival), Israel, Kenya, Laos, Madagascar, Malaysia, Marshall Islands, Myanmar, Namibia, Nicaragua, Nigeria, Oman, Palau, Papua New Guinea, Philippines, Rwanda, Saint Lucia, Samoa, Saudi Arabia, Singapore, Solomon Islands, Sri Lanka, Suriname, Taiwan, Tanzania, Timor-Leste, Tonga, Tuvalu, Uganda, Vanuatu, Venezuela, Vietnam, countries requiring passport validity of at least 4 months on arrival include Micronesia, Zambia, countries requiring passport validity of at least 3 months on arrival include Georgia, Honduras, Iceland, Jordan, Kuwait, Lebanon, Moldova, Nauru, Panama, United Arab Emirates and countries requiring passport validity of at least 1 month on arrival include Eritrea, Hong Kong, Macao, New Zealand, South Africa.[320] Other countries require either a passport valid on arrival or passport valid throughout the period of intended stay.

Non-ordinary passports[edit]

Holders of various categories of official Maltese passports have additional visa-free access to the following countries - China (diplomatic or service passports), Libya (diplomatic, official or service passports) and Russia (diplomatic passports). Holders of diplomatic or service passports of any country have visa-free access to Cape Verde, Ethiopia, Mali and Zimbabwe.

Right to consular protection in non-EU countries[edit]

When in a non-EU country where there is no Maltese embassy, Maltese citizens as EU citizens have the right to get consular protection from the embassy of any other EU country present in that country.
